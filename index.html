1.1.Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting is a way whereby LLMs come up with a series of reasoning steps which precede their output. It trains the model to speak more intermediate logic, helps to reason with transparency and with structure. As a result, the method decreases error rate ‚Äì especially in arithmetic tasks, logical conclusions, or any problem that needs several steps of reasoning. However, although CoT enhances the level of reasoning, it also exposes a considerable weakness in‚Ä¶ verification. Knowledge that AI system can establish and maintain is only as much as it sees knowledge to be verifiable. Current studies in long-form Chain-of-Thought reasoning suggest much promise regarding its ability to address competitive and complex issues, but projects shallow and underdeveloped verification effort in the current models.

The main goal of this study is to highlight the strengths and weaknesses of CoT and simple prompt approaches.
